Key Insights

What the data tells us.

Six product-level findings derived from SQL analysis of the sensor dataset.

01
🌡️

Temperature Spike Detected

Sensor EM500PT100_84553 recorded a peak of 98.3°C — far outside expected cold-chain range. This likely signals a sensor malfunction or a genuine thermal event needing immediate investigation.

02
🔋

Critical Battery Failure

Sensor EM300TH_50743 has a battery level of 1% — effectively dead. All 1,631 readings from this sensor are at risk of being lost. Immediate replacement recommended.

03
🚪

Peak Door Access: 3–5 PM

Door open events peak between 15:00–17:00 with 105 and 100 events respectively. This aligns with typical shift-change windows and suggests a product opportunity for scheduled access reminders.

04
📊

5,678 Anomaly Events

Over 24.8% of all readings triggered at least one alert condition. EM300MCS_21808 alone accounts for 3,003 alerts — its consistently near-zero temperature suggests it may be monitoring a different zone.

05
💧

Humidity Data Gaps

Over 44% of humidity readings are null — primarily because probe-type sensors (EM500PT100) don't measure humidity. Product recommendation: add humidity-capable sensors to probe zones for full environmental coverage.

06
❄️

Cold Chain Integrity

Cold storage sensors (EM300MCS) maintain an average of -11.6°C to -7.5°C, within expected range. However, temperature spikes up to +20°C during door-open events indicate a need for door-seal inspection.

A repeatable product analytics workflow — from raw CSV to actionable insights.
